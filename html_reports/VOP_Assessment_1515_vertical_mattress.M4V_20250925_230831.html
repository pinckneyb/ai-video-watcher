Summative Assessment:
The operator performed a methodical, competent vertical mattress closure with consistent pass symmetry, reliable needle control, and precise forceps-assisted tissue presentation. Passes were perpendicular-to-oblique with regular spacing, instrument ties were uniform and secure, and knots were managed with short tails; the workflow remained steady and focused on the single incision throughout. Tension balance was well maintained — deep support provided robust approximation while superficial components closed the epidermis without overt strangulation, yielding mild, uniform eversion. Overall this is a proficient execution of vertical mattress suturing appropriate for secure, cosmetically acceptable skin approximation; minor inefficiencies (infrequent hand crossings and a brief remote movement) did not compromise technique or wound integrity.
