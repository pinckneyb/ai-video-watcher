2. Gentle tissue handling
Tissue handling is atraumatic with precise, limited forceps grasps and minimal manipulation per bite; the operator uses fingertip counter‑traction appropriately. There is no evidence of repetitive crushing or excessive regrasping.

Score: 3/5 - Competent

Motion economy is excellent—hands and instruments remain close to the wound, motions are efficient and rhythmic, and there are minimal extraneous movements or repositioning. Workflow is methodical and time‑efficient.

Summative Assessment:
The closure demonstrates proficient vertical mattress technique with consistent pass symmetry, controlled needle arcs, and uniform spacing. Tissue handling is atraumatic and deliberate, producing reliable epidermal apposition with a sustained slight eversion and no evidence of edge inversion or overtightening. Knots are secure and neatly trimmed, contributing to a tidy, reproducible suture line. Tension balance between deep support and superficial approximation is well managed, with the deep component providing structural apposition and the superficial component avoiding strangulation of the epidermis. Instrument control and economy of motion are strong, yielding an efficient, high‑quality repair appropriate for clinical practice. Minor refinements could elevate this to exemplary, but the performance is fundamentally competent and reliable.
