Tension is well balanced between deep support and superficial approximation — deep components appear to bear closure force while superficial throws produce gentle epidermal apposition without blanching or gaping. Overall tension distribution yields stable approximation.

Summative Assessment:
The operator executed the vertical mattress closure with consistent technique: controlled mid‑shaft needle grips, precise two‑instrument choreography, and reliably perpendicular passes producing uniform spacing and effective deep versus superficial support. Knot tying was systematic and secure, tails were managed and trimmed, and the overall line shows modest, appropriate eversion without edge inversion or gap formation. Tissue handling and motion economy were strong — forceps use was precise, instrument transfers were controlled, and hand positioning adapted smoothly to lateral and medial segments. The repair meets a high standard of competent vertical mattress execution suitable for clinical dermal approximation, with only minor inefficiencies in instrument resting that did not affect technical quality.
