Rubric Assessment

1. Correct deep (far-far) and superficial (near-near) passes
Deep (far‑far) and superficial (near‑near) components were executed in a consistent, methodical pattern with needle passes perpendicular to the wound edges and appropriate dermal purchase producing reliable layer apposition. Entry/exit points were symmetric and aligned along the incision.

2. Gentle tissue handling
Tissue handling was controlled with the forceps regularly presenting and everting the near edge with single, precise grasps; occasional brief instrument exchanges did not result in repeated traumatic grabs. Overall soft, deliberate manipulation minimized unnecessary handling.
Score: 3/5 - Competent
3. Square, secure knots
Knots were consistently constructed, sequentially tightened, and seated with appropriate security; throws appeared neat and maintained tension without evidence of slippage or over‑cinching. Knot placement was close to the skin surface and uniform across stitches.
Score: 3/5 - Competent

Tension balance between deep support and superficial closure was well managed—the closure demonstrated firm underlying support with superficial bites approximating skin without strangulation. Occasional small adjustments were made to avoid over‑tightening.

Spacing between interrupted sutures was uniform and within the expected 0.5–1.0 cm range, creating an even line of closure with consistent interval symmetry.
Score: 3/5 - Competent
6. Proper eversion
Eversion was consistently achieved at each stitch, producing a slight raised ridge and preventing inversion of the epidermal edges across the incision.
Score: 3/5 - Competent

Motions were economical and focused—hands and instruments remained close to the wound with minimal departures, efficient instrument exchanges, and steady rhythm from placement through knot tying.

Summative Assessment:
The closure demonstrated proficient vertical mattress technique with consistent pass symmetry, controlled needle handling, and reliable eversion.
