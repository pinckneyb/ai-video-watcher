Summative Assessment:
You maintained disciplined needle control with perpendicular entries, short rotational arcs, and consistent dermal bite depth and spacing. Forceps use was protective and precise, producing reliable eversion and clean edge presentation. The line advanced in a structured, methodical cadence from inferior to superior with good suture management and triangulation, yielding uniform approximation without gapping. The principal deficiency is the finish: knots were repeatedly externalized along the working line. For a subcuticular closure, knots must be buried (or the line concluded with an approved subcuticular end-loop) to keep the construct within the dermal plane. Correct this by burying the start and end appropriately and avoiding external knots mid-line. Efficiency would further improve by preventing strand drape and reducing hand swaps, but overall handling was atraumatic, knots were secure and square, and the final contour was nearly flat.
