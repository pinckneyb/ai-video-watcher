Summative Assessment:
The operator demonstrated a competent, controlled vertical mattress closure with reliable pass symmetry, consistent spacing, and excellent tissue handling. Needle control and mid‑shaft gripping produced predictable perpendicular entries; instrument choreography maintained fine control and minimized trauma. Knots were tied deliberately and seated securely, with appropriate tail management. Tension balance between deep and superficial components was well managed—deep support without tissue strangulation and superficial throws providing uniform slight eversion for optimal epidermal apposition. Overall the performance reflects proficient technique suitable for clinical skin closure, with only minor opportunities for refinement in speed and micro‑efficiency of knot transitions.
