Summative Assessment:
The operator performed an orderly, well-controlled vertical mattress closure with consistent pass symmetry, reliable perpendicular needle trajectories, and uniform spacing. Tissue handling was precise and atraumatic, instrument coordination between needle driver and forceps was excellent, and knots were consistently managed to maintain secure approximation. Tension balance between the deep and superficial components was appropriate—deep support held the wound without over-tightening while superficial throws achieved close epidermal apposition with mild eversion. Overall technique is proficient and efficient; minor localized puckering was recognized and actively mitigated, but vigilance is required to avoid cumulative tissue bunching when placing adjacent stitches.
