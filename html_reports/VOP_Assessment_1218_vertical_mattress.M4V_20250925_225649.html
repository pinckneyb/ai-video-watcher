Summative Assessment:
The operator performed a competent vertical mattress closure with consistent pass symmetry, predictable needle control, and uniform spacing. Tissue handling was atraumatic and deliberate, with fine forceps used to evert and present edges appropriately; knot construction and trimming were reliable and reproducible. Tensioning between deep support and superficial approximation was well balanced, avoiding epidermal strangulation while maintaining firm wound apposition. Overall the performance demonstrates proficient technical execution and efficient workflow suitable for clinical skin closure. Minor variations in grip position and brief instrument crossings did not compromise coaptation, eversion, or knot security; the closure is stable, evenly everted, and cosmetically acceptable.
