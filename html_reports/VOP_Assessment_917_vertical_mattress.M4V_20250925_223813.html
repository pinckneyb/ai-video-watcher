Surgical VOP Assessment Report

Video: 917_vertical_mattress.M4V

Pattern: Vertical Mattress

Rubric Assessment

1. Correct deep (far-far) and superficial (near-near) passes
Deep (far‑far) and superficial (near‑near) passes appear to be executed in a controlled, repeatable manner with perpendicular-to-oblique needle trajectories and consistent use of the needle curve to traverse tissue. Overall support and epidermal approximation are acceptable though some bites show mild asymmetry.
Score: 2/5 - Novice
2. Gentle tissue handling
Tissue handling is precise with coordinated driver–forceps choreography, minimal unnecessary grasps and deliberate transfers; occasional repositioning of forceps and needle grips occurs but does not cause tissue trauma.
Score: 3/5 - Competent
3. Square, secure knots
Knots are consistently formed, seated snugly without obvious slippage and tails are trimmed; a few tails are slightly long but overall knot security and construction are reliable.
Score: 3/5 - Competent
4. Balanced tension deep vs superficial
Tension balance between the deep and superficial components is generally appropriate with deep support and superficial closure avoiding strangulation, though mild variability produced intermittent slight gapping or puckering at some stitches.
Score: 2/5 - Novice
5. Even spacing (0.5-1.0 cm)
Spacing is mostly uniform within the expected 0.5–1.0 cm range, but several sutures show modest inconsistency leading to small gaps between adjacent bites.
Score: 2/5 - Novice
6. Proper eversion
Eversion is achieved across much of the line with a visible raised ridge and edge apposition; eversion quality is modestly inconsistent with a few sutures showing under- or over‑elevation.
Score: 3/5 - Competent
7. Economy of time and motion
Motion economy is good — hands and instruments remain close to the wound in a tight working zone, minimal extraneous movements and efficient progression along the incision.
Score: 3/5 - Competent
Average Score: 2.6/5
Summative Assessment:
The operator demonstrates competent vertical mattress technique with reliable instrument control, coordinated driver‑forceps use, and secure knot construction. Pass symmetry and perpendicular needle trajectories are generally maintained, producing acceptable epidermal approximation and modest edge eversion for most stitches. Deficiencies are technical rather than fundamental: spacing and bite depth show intermittent variability leading to small gaps and occasional localized puckering, and a few knots have slightly long tails. With tighter attention to uniform bite placement and consistent tensioning of the superficial component the closure would reach a higher, more reproducible standard.
